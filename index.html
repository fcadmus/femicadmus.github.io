About

Leading Innovation in Legal Information

My career spans over three decades in academic law librarianship, including leadership positions at Cornell, Duke, and Yale. I served as President of the American Association of Law Libraries (2018-2019) and remain actively engaged in promoting open access legal scholarship, technology in legal practice, access to legal information and access to justice globally.

I am Founder of Law Archive and a Co-founder of LawArXiv, pioneering free open access global legal scholarship repositories. I have served on several boards including the Yale Law Journal Company Inc. and the Yale Law Journal Fund Inc. as Treasurer. Admitted to practice law in New York, I hold an LL.M. in Law in Development from the University of Warwick, England.

Transformative Leadership in Legal Education

Leading legal information centers at three of the nation's most prestigious law schools, driving innovation in legal research, pedagogy, and technology integration while advancing open access to legal scholarship globally.

Yale Law School (2021-2026): Law Librarian & Professor of Law, providing strategic directions, operational oversight, and leadership of the Lillian Goldman Law Library with approximately 35 staff members and $9+ million budget. Teaching Technology in the Practice of Law and Advanced Legal Research.

Duke Law School (2018-2021): Archibald C. and Frances Fulk Rufty Distinguished Research Professor of Law, Associate Dean, Information Services & Technology & Director, J. Michael Goodson Law Library. Providing strategic direction and leadership to a cross-functional organization of over 30 personnel.

Cornell Law School (2011-2018): Edward Cornell Law Librarian, Associate Dean for Library Services & Professor of the Practice, delivering transformational leadership to a high-performing organization of 21.
